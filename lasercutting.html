2D CNC Cutting

Introduction

2D CNC cutting, also known as Computer Numerical Control cutting, is a manufacturing process that uses computer-controlled machines to cut and shape various materials, such as metal, wood, or plastic. It offers precise and automated cutting capabilities, making it popular in industries like aerospace, automotive, and signage.

The Process

The process of 2D CNC cutting involves several steps:

  1. Design: A CAD (Computer-Aided Design) software is used to create a digital design or drawing of the desired shape or pattern.
  2. Conversion: The CAD design is converted into a format compatible with CNC machines, such as G-code.
  3. Machine Setup: The material to be cut is secured onto the CNC machine, and the cutting tool is properly positioned.
  4. Execution: The CNC machine follows the instructions from the G-code and moves the cutting tool along the programmed path to cut the material.
  5. Finishing: After the cutting process is complete, the material may require additional finishing operations, such as deburring or polishing.

Advantages

2D CNC cutting offers several advantages over traditional manual cutting methods:

  • Precision: CNC machines can achieve high levels of accuracy and repeatability, ensuring consistent and precise cuts.
  • Speed: With automated cutting, CNC machines can work much faster than manual methods, reducing production time.
  • Complex Shapes: CNC cutting allows for the creation of intricate and complex shapes that may be difficult or impossible to achieve manually.
  • Efficiency: The automation of the cutting process increases efficiency and reduces the risk of human error.
『 ☆ Laser Cutting Machines ☆ 』
Laser Cutting Machines

Laser Cutting Machines

CO2 Laser Cutting Machine

  • This type of machine uses a carbon dioxide gas laser to generate the laser beam.
  • It is versatile and suitable for cutting materials like wood, acrylic, plastics, textiles, and some metals.

Fiber Laser Cutting Machine

  • Fiber lasers use optical fibers to amplify the laser beam.
  • They are highly efficient and primarily used for cutting metals, including steel, aluminium, brass, and copper.

Nd:YAG Laser Cutting Machine

  • Nd:YAG (neodymium-doped yttrium aluminium garnet) lasers produce high-energy pulsed laser beams.
  • They are mainly used for cutting thin metals and are known for their precision and speed.
『 ☆ Laser Cutting Settings ☆ 』

Power

  • Higher power cuts deeper/thicker material.
  • However, higher power may also cause material melting.

Speed

  • Higher speed moves the laser head faster over the material, resulting in shallow cuts.
  • Too low speeds may cause material melting.

Frequency

  • Use higher frequency for denser materials.

The rule of thumb is that power, speed, and frequency should be adjusted in relation to the types of materials. Always follow the recommended settings provided by the material sheet for reference!

『 ☆ Create a keychain for laser cutting ☆ 』

Specifications:

  • Size: 30mm x 70mm
  • Thickness: 3mm

Steps to Create a Keychain in Fusion 360:

  1. Create a new design file and set the workspace to "Model".
  2. Import the Fablab logo into your design.
  3. Adjust the dimensions of the logo to fit the keychain size.
  4. Extrude the logo to a thickness of 3mm.
  5. Add any additional text or design elements to the keychain.
  6. Export the design as an STL file.
  7. Prepare the STL file for 3D printing using slicing software.
  8. Send the sliced file to the 3D printer and start the printing process.
  9. Remove the printed keychain from the printer and clean it if necessary.
  10. Your keychain is ready to use!
